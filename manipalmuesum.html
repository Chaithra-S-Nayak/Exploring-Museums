MANIPAL MUSEUM OF ANATOMY AND PATHOLOGY (MAP)

About Museum:

Manipal Museum of Anatomy and Pathology featuring human and animal specimens including skeletons and diseased organs is a sprawling building which is located in Manipal, an educational and banking town near Udupi in coastal Karnataka .

This museum is a wonderful learning aid for students and for visitors it is a storehouse for knowledge.

MAP houses 3000+ specimens and samples related to anatomical , including the skulls of an elephant and whale, and the long skeleton of king cobra.

The specimens have been there for close to 40 years. The renovated museum with its new magnificent ambiance will be an added attraction for hundreds and thousands of visitors.

HISTORY:

Opened to the public in 1954, Manipal Museum of Anatomy & Pathology is the idea and innovation of Dr. SS Godbole.

Dr. SS Godbole was first Anatomy Professor at KMC (Kasturba Medical College) in Manipal.He carefully crafted process for dissecting; processing and mounting dead creatures.

Dr Godbole has donated over 650 specimens from his personal collection for the museum.

Apart from displaying body parts of creatures, their structure, The Museum of Anatomy & Pathology has a dedicated section on lifestyle induced disease.

This museum was renamed as Manipal Museum of Anatomy and Pathology in November 2012.

The specimen which we see even now at the museum are preserved almost for 40 years.

R.K. Pachauri, Director-General of The Energy and Resources Institute (TERI), New Delhi, and the former Chief Justice of India M.N. Venkatachaliah inaugurated the renovated Museum of Anatomy and Pathology (MAP) in year 2012.

The museum is divided into two main sections:

Anatomy

Pathology

ANATOMY:

The anatomy section displays specimens of normal human body parts and organs in a manner that makes them easy to understand. Each organ system of the human body can be explored as a separate entity. Every bit of the body is displayed from head to toe from different sections.

There is a section on comparative anatomy, where a large collection of animals, their skeleton, and bones are displayed.

Cardiovascular system:

The circulatory system , also called the cardiovascular system .

It is the organ system which permits the blood to circulate and transport nutrients , oxygen ,carbon dioxide and blood cells to and from the cells in the body.

EMBRYOLOGY:

Embryology is the branch of biology that studies the prenatal development of gametes(sex cells), Fertilization , and development of embryos and foetus.

REPRODUCTIVE SYSTEM:

The reproductive system of an organism , also known as the genital system is the biological system made up of all the anatomical organs involved in sexual reproduction.

EXCRETORY SYSTEM:

The excretory system is a passive biological system that removes excess, unnecessary materials from the body fluids of an organism, so as to help maintain internal chemical homeostasis and prevent damage to the body.

PATHOLOGY:

Pathology is the medical discipline that provides diagnostic information to patients and clinicians. It impacts nearly all aspects of patient care, from diagnosing cancer to managing chronic diseases through accurate laboratory testing.

The Pathology Museum, which displays diseased body parts and organs, has a popular section on life-style related diseases, and their impact on the human body. Work to convert it into a digital or virtual museum is completed.
